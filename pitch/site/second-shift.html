What a pack is

A turnkey department, under maker-checker discipline.

A pack is a department of specialist agents. Each one drafts and recommends; a credentialed human reviews and commits. 1,118 agents across 32 packs, ready to switch on.

01

A fixed job

Every agent has one bounded persona and one task — a 13-week cash forecaster, a 1099 extractor, a citation verifier. No open-ended prompt to engineer.

02

Least privilege

Each agent gets a least-privilege tool set and a risk ceiling it cannot exceed — a narrow allow-list, an explicit deny-list, bound to every action.

03

A human signs off

Agents draft and recommend; they don't commit. Work routes to a credentialed reviewer who certifies and releases it — maker-checker, built in.

Office of the CFO

Tax for CPA firms

A documents-to-draft workflow: classify and extract W-2, the 1099 family, K-1, and 1098 into a standardized workpaper, then a first-pass 1040 plus the resident-state return. Every line cites its source document. It never files.
