The thesis

Agents will do enterprise work. The question is whose governance they run through.

Frontier labs build smarter agents — that race is commoditizing by the month. The hard part, and the durable one, is the layer around the agent: governance bound to every action, an audit trail that can't be forged, and a workforce that improves under proof. That's what we build, and we built it as real infrastructure rather than a prompt over an API.

Founder

Christopher Day

Christopher Day is the founder of Daybreak Labs. He built Lightwork — the runtime, the governance, and the 1,118 specialist packs — because the thing standing between AI agents and real regulated work was never the model. It was the layer around it: governance bound to every action, an audit trail that can't be forged, and proof the system improves instead of quietly drifting.

He started Daybreak Labs to build that layer as real infrastructure rather than a prompt over an API — and to put it in the hands of the finance, healthcare, insurance, and legal teams who can't ship an agent until they can stand behind every decision it makes.
