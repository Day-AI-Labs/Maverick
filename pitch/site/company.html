The thesis

Agents will do enterprise work. The question is whose governance they run through.

Frontier labs build smarter agents — that race is commoditizing by the month. The hard part, and the durable one, is the layer around the agent: governance bound to every action, an audit trail that can't be forged, and a workforce that improves under proof. That's what we build, and we built it as real infrastructure rather than a prompt over an API.

Founders

A builder and a governance leader.

Christopher Day

Founder

Christopher Day founded Daybreak Labs and built Lightwork — the runtime, the governance, and the 1,118 specialist packs. He started it on a simple conviction: the thing standing between AI agents and real regulated work was never the model, it was the layer around it — governance bound to every action, an audit trail that can't be forged, and proof the system improves instead of drifting. So he built that layer as real infrastructure, not a prompt over an API.

Andrew Bjerken

Co-Founder

Andrew Bjerken is the governance and trust half of the company — the executive who has spent a career being the one who has to sign off. Today he leads global privacy and AI governance at a Fortune-scale enterprise; before that he was a Global CISO and Chief Privacy Officer through restructurings, regulators, and board rooms. His path runs from cyber operations and red-teaming in the U.S. Air Force through security leadership at ADP and Reckitt, and onto the IAPP advisory board. He works the exact question Lightwork answers — how to let agents act while execution authority, human oversight, and evidence stay where regulators expect them. His conviction, and the company's: trust is not a control function, it is an enterprise capability.
