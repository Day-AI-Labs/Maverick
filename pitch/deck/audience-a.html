The problem

Every enterprise wants an AI agent workforce.
Almost none can deploy one where it matters.

In regulated, high-stakes work the blocker isn’t capability — it’s governance, auditability, and trust.

No proof it stayed in policy

No tamper-evident record of what an agent did. No way to prove it’s getting better, not quietly drifting.

A forced bad trade

Hosted black boxes you can’t run on your own data — or ungoverned frameworks you have to secure yourself.

Where it dies

The demo wins. Then the CISO, compliance, and GC ask “show me what it did, prove it can’t exfiltrate, prove it won’t get worse” — and it stalls.

Agentic AI is stuck in pilots — in exactly the industries that would pay the most for it.

Every single action passes one chokepoint — then lands where a human signs off. Self-host or air-gap, on the customer’s data.
