# Ambassadors program — charter

**Roadmap ref:** 2027-H2 Distribution — "ambassadors program".
**Status:** charter complete; opening nominations is the remaining
operational work. **Cohort size and any stipend are founder decisions.**

Ambassadors are practitioners who already do the work — answer questions,
run meetups, write honest material — and get recognized and equipped for it.
The title follows the behavior; it is never a growth-hacking job posting.
Because Maverick is proprietary, ambassadors are *community* voices, not a
sales channel: they are never asked to pitch licenses.

## Criteria (all required)

1. **Demonstrated contribution over ≥3 months** — any mix of: merged PRs
   (under the [CLA](../../CLA.md)), high-quality issue triage, published
   skills/plugins that pass the validator and
   [moderation checks](./skill-of-the-year.md), a verified
   [showcase-wall](../showcase.md) entry, meetup organizing
   ([`meetups.md`](./meetups.md)), or teaching material that meets the
   claims policy (grounded in [`FEATURES.md`](../FEATURES.md)).
2. **Technical fluency** — can install, configure, and demo the runtime
   unassisted (the [booth kit](./conference-booth.md) station list is the
   practical bar).
3. **Conduct record** — no unresolved [code-of-conduct](../../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md)
   issues. Ambassadors are held to the CoC's *community leader* standard:
   they model it, and a violation removes the title (see below).
4. **Honesty fit** — their public material doesn't over-claim. One review
   of their existing content before acceptance.

Selection: self-nomination or maintainer invitation; maintainer decides.
Cohort size: small (_founder-set; single digits recommended_). Term: 12
months, renewable on the same criteria.

## Perks

| Perk | Detail |
|---|---|
| Recognition | Named on a docs-site ambassadors page; ambassador-tier badge per [`badge-program.md`](./badge-program.md) |
| Access | A private channel with the maintainer; pre-release notes at tag time (no embargoed secrets — the repo is the source of truth) |
| Event support | Meetup kit + costs per [`meetups.md`](./meetups.md); summit speaking-slot priority (content still vets on merit) |
| Swag | Contributor-grade gift per [`swag.md`](./swag.md) |
| License for demos | A written demo/evaluation license grant from the Licensor covering their ambassador activities — required because Maverick is proprietary; scope is a **founder decision** |
| Stipend | _Founder decision; default none — this is recognition, not employment_ |

## Obligations (kept light)

- Stay active: roughly one community act per quarter (a meetup, a talk, a
  tutorial, sustained triage). No quotas beyond that.
- **Disclose the affiliation** whenever speaking about Maverick.
- Follow the claims policy (capabilities from `FEATURES.md`, forward-looking
  statements labeled as roadmap, no open-source promises — the lite edition
  is a stated possibility, not a commitment) and
  [`TRADEMARK.md`](../../TRADEMARK.md) (nominative use; never "official").
- Route press to the [press kit](./press-and-case-studies.md) contact;
  route license questions to the licensor contact; route security reports
  to [`SECURITY.md`](../../SECURITY.md). Ambassadors never speak *for* the
  project on those three.

## Code-of-conduct tie-in

The ambassador title makes someone a *community leader* under
[`CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md`](../../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md), with the enforcement
responsibilities that section assigns. Consequences for violations follow
the CoC's enforcement guidelines, with one addition: any enforcement action
at the *warning* level or above suspends the title pending review; a
*temporary ban* or above removes it. Conduct reports follow the existing
channel (private security advisory with `[CONDUCT]` in the title, per
[`MAINTAINERS.md`](../../MAINTAINERS.md)).

## Lifecycle + measurement

Annual renewal against the same criteria; lapsing is normal and unceremonious
(people get busy — thank them, remove the row). Track per ambassador-year:
events run, material published, PRs/triage touched. The program succeeds if
ambassadors collectively run more community surface than the maintainer
could alone; it fails (and should shrink) if managing it costs more
maintainer time than it saves.
