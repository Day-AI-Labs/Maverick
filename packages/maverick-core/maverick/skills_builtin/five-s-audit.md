# What this skill does

Audits a defined work area against the 5S pillars (Sort, Set in Order, Shine, Standardize, Sustain) using a consistent rubric, then scores each pillar and aggregates an area score. Produces a 5S audit with per-pillar scores, evidence-backed findings, and prioritized corrective actions with owners.

# Steps

1. Confirm the audit scope (specific area/cell) and load the organization's 5S checklist and prior audit baseline via `knowledge_search`. If no standard rubric exists, use a five-question-per-pillar scale (0-5 each, 25 total) and state that the rubric is the default, not the customer's.
2. Score each pillar against its criteria with a concrete observation per item — Sort (only needed items present), Set in Order (everything has a marked location), Shine (clean, no leaks/contamination), Standardize (visual standards exist and are visible), Sustain (audits/habits hold over time). Record what was seen; do not infer a score without evidence.
3. Aggregate the pillar scores into an area total and percentage, and compare against the prior baseline to show trend (improving/holding/regressing).
4. List corrective actions for every item scored below threshold, each with a suggested owner and target date, ranked by safety and quick-win impact. Hand off the scored audit; state assumptions (rubric source, who was present, areas not accessible during the walk) and mark any item not directly observed.

# Notes

The audit is wrong if scores are given without on-the-floor evidence, if "Sustain" is rated high on a first audit (it can only be earned over repeated cycles), or if the scope silently expands mid-walk so the baseline comparison is invalid. Photos or specific observations should back low scores; vague findings produce disputed actions. Not a substitute for a safety audit — flag hazards but route them to the safety process. The audit and proposed actions are recommendations; owner assignments and any disposal of "Sorted" items must be confirmed by the area lead before action, as discarding tooling or stock is irreversible.
