# What this skill does

Prepares a team for an industry-analyst briefing (Gartner, Forrester, IDC, etc.). Produces an AR brief with the core narrative, proof points tied to the analyst's evaluation criteria, a profile of the analyst's coverage, and anticipated Q&A with prepared responses.

# Steps

1. Profile the engagement via knowledge_search: the analyst/firm, their coverage area and recent reports (e.g., relevant Magic Quadrant or Wave), the briefing objective, and the product story to be told. Note the analyst's known POV and prior positions; mark as unverified if not sourced.
2. Construct the narrative arc — market problem, our positioning, differentiation, momentum — mapped to the criteria the analyst is known to weight. Keep every claim grounded in approved facts.
3. Assemble proof points for each narrative beat: customer outcomes, metrics, roadmap evidence, references. Cite the internal source for each; flag any claim lacking proof rather than asserting it.
4. Anticipate hard questions (competitive gaps, pricing, scale, roadmap risk) and draft honest, defensible responses; note which answers need a SME or must stay off-record.
5. Report the brief as narrative + proof table + Q&A, plus a pre-brief checklist (who presents which section, materials to send, follow-ups to commit). State assumptions about the analyst's stance.

# Notes

The brief is wrong if proof points are inflated, competitor claims are unsubstantiated, or the analyst's expected criteria are guessed without sourcing — analysts probe and remember overstatements. Mark unverified analyst positions and any roadmap item as directional, not committed. The brief is preparation for a human-led briefing; it does not authorize claims or disclosures — sensitive or forward-looking statements need approval before they are said aloud.
