# What this skill does

Builds an account-specific plan to displace an incumbent competitor: the
business case for switching, the competitor's known traps, the technical and
contractual migration path, and the sequenced plays to execute. Produces a
displacement plan a deal team can run against a named account and incumbent.

# Steps

1. Establish the target account and incumbent from the request. Use
   `knowledge_search` to retrieve the incumbent's battlecard, prior win/loss
   notes against them, and the account's stated pain or renewal timing.
2. Map the switching cost honestly — contract lock-in, integrations, retraining,
   data migration. Identify the wedge: the specific pain the incumbent cannot
   fix and the event (renewal, outage, exec change) that opens the window.
3. List the incumbent's competitive traps (FUD, lock-in clauses, bundled
   discounts) and the documented counter for each; mark any counter you cannot
   source as unverified rather than asserting it.
4. Sequence the plays: land a beachhead use case, prove value, build the
   migration runbook, and stage the contractual switch. Hand off the plan with
   owners and timing, flagging that the displacement decision and any contract
   action are the customer's and the AE's to commit.

# Notes

Output is wrong if it underestimates switching cost or invents incumbent
weaknesses without a battlecard or interview behind them — credibility dies on
one fabricated claim. A displacement with no triggering event is wishful;
require a real window. This is a recommended plan; the customer signs and the
deal owner commits resources. Do not use for greenfield accounts (no incumbent
to displace) or when the relationship is too early to know the account's pains.
