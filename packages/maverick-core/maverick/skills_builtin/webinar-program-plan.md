# What this skill does

Plans a webinar or virtual-event program end to end: topic and audience framing, content/agenda, a dated promotion sequence, day-of run-of-show, and a post-event follow-up flow with conversion goals. Produces a single plan a marketer can execute, grounded in the organization's actual products, audience, and prior event performance rather than generic best practice.

# Steps

1. Establish the goal and audience with `knowledge_search` over internal context: which segment/persona, what product or message this supports, the target registrations and attendance rate, and any results from past webinars. State the goal as a measurable target (registrations, attendees, qualified follow-ups), and mark any number you assumed vs. found.
2. Define content: a working title, 3-5 agenda beats, speaker roles, and format (live demo, panel, fireside). Ground the topic in real product capability and known audience pain pulled from internal sources; do not invent claims, customer names, or stats.
3. Build the promotion timeline backward from the event date: email sequence (announce, reminders, last-chance), owned/social posts, partner or paid amplification, and a registration page checklist. Include a realistic registration-to-attendance assumption and state it.
4. Specify day-of run-of-show (intros, content blocks, Q&A, CTA) and the follow-up flow: recording send, attended vs no-show split, lead scoring/handoff to sales, and the conversion goal. Report the full plan with assumptions listed; flag where live data (list size, sender reputation) is needed to finalize, and stage any sends for human approval.

# Notes

The plan is wrong if it asserts unverified product features, fabricates customer proof, or assumes a list size/attendance rate without grounding — mark every such assumption. Registration-to-attendance varies widely by audience and channel; treat any rate as a planning estimate, not a promise. This produces a draft program, not scheduled sends or live invites — a human approves outreach, speakers, and any compliance-sensitive content. Do not use for purely paid-media campaigns with no live-event component.
