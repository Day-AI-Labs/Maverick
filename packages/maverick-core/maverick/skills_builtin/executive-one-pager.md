# What this skill does

Compresses a body of analysis into a single BLUF (bottom-line-up-front) page structured as ask, context, options, recommendation, risks, and next step, so a busy decision-maker can act without reading the full work. The goal class is "brief a leader in one page": lead with the conclusion and the decision being requested, then support it tightly.

# Steps

1. Read the full analysis with read_file and extract the single most important conclusion and the specific decision or resource being asked for.
2. Open with the BLUF: state the ask and the recommendation in the first two sentences. A reader who stops there should still know what you want and what you think.
3. Provide just enough context to make the recommendation legible, lay out the realistic options (including do-nothing), and state the recommendation with its rationale.
4. Close with the material risks (and how they are mitigated) and a concrete next step with an owner. Keep the whole thing to one page; cut anything that does not change the decision.

# Notes

Burying the lede is the cardinal sin of executive writing — the decision and recommendation go first, not in a conclusion at the bottom. Including every fact you found dilutes the signal; ruthlessly cut detail that does not move the decision (link to the full analysis instead). Presenting only the recommended option with no alternatives reads as advocacy, not analysis; always show the options considered. This skill drafts the brief for the author/approver; it does not distribute it or make the decision.
