# What this skill does

Designs an incentive compensation plan for a specific sales role: sets quota, the base/variable OTE mix, commission rates, and accelerators/decelerators, then models payout across performance scenarios. Produces a plan document plus a payout model that finance and sales leadership review before rollout. Handles the recurring task of translating a role's targets and budget into a motivating, affordable comp structure.

# Steps

1. Establish the inputs: role (AE, SDR, CSM), target OTE and base/variable split, annual quota, deal economics (ACV, sales cycle, margin), and the comp-cost-of-sales budget cap. If quota or OTE is unstated, treat it as a required input and stop — do not invent target numbers.
2. In a `spreadsheet`, define the plan mechanics: measure(s) (bookings, new logos, retention), commission rate to hit 100% variable at quota, accelerator tiers above quota, any decelerator/cliff below threshold, and caps or decelerators on windfalls. Tie SDR/CSM measures to their actual lever, not raw revenue.
3. Model payout curves across scenarios (e.g., 50/80/100/120/150% of quota): compute earnings, blended commission rate, and total comp cost as a percent of bookings. Verify on-target cost stays within the budget cap and that accelerators reward over-performance without breaking the cost-of-sales ceiling.
4. Deliver the plan: quota, OTE and mix, rate table with accelerators/thresholds, payout-curve chart, and the cost-of-sales model. Report assumptions (ramp, attainment distribution, deal size) and hand off to sales leadership and finance for approval before any rep is enrolled.

# Notes

Output is wrong if it pays above the cost-of-sales cap at expected attainment, creates perverse incentives (sandbagging, discount-driven deals, ignoring retention), or omits guardrails on outlier deals. The plan is a recommendation: leadership and finance approve it, and changing an active rep's comp is an irreversible commitment staged for human sign-off — never auto-applied. State every modeling assumption explicitly; do not present a single attainment scenario as the expected outcome. Do not use to adjudicate individual commission disputes or compute an actual payroll run.
