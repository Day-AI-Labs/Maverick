# What this skill does

Drafts a publishable customer case study in the standard challenge / solution / results arc from existing source material (interview notes, CRM outcomes, support history, product usage). Output is a structured narrative with a headline, a customer profile sidebar, quantified results, and at least one attributable quote — every fact traceable to a source.

# Steps

1. Gather source material with `knowledge_search`: customer interview transcript or notes, the original problem statement, the deployed solution/products, and measured outcomes (metrics, before/after, timeframe). List which sources back which claims; flag any results that are projections or estimates rather than measured.
2. Write the Challenge: the customer's pain in their own framing — context, what they tried, the cost of inaction. Ground every detail in a source; do not invent stakes or numbers.
3. Write the Solution and Results: what was deployed and how it was adopted, then quantified outcomes (percent/absolute, with the measurement window). Attach a real quote to a named role; mark any quote that has not been approved for publication as DRAFT-UNAPPROVED.
4. Assemble the deliverable — headline, profile sidebar (industry, size, region), the three sections, a results stat block, and a quote — and hand off, stating which metrics need customer/legal sign-off and which claims are unverified.

# Notes

The output is wrong if it fabricates metrics, attributes an unapproved quote, or inflates results beyond what the source supports — published case studies carry legal and brand risk. Mark every number with its source and measurement window; never round a "we think it helped" into a hard percentage. This skill produces a DRAFT for human and customer approval; publication is the human's irreversible decision. Do not use when the only available outcome is anecdotal with no metric and no quotable contact — escalate for an interview first.
