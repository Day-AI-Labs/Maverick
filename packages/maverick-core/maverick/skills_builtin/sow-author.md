# What this skill does

Authors a client-ready Statement of Work for a defined engagement. Produces a structured SOW covering scope, deliverables, milestones with dates, assumptions/exclusions, and explicit acceptance criteria — grounded in the actual deal terms and prior SOW precedent, not boilerplate.

# Steps

1. Gather the engagement inputs from the requester or the deal record: parties, objective, in-scope work, term/timeline, commercial model (fixed-fee vs T&M), and named stakeholders. Flag any of these that are missing rather than inventing them.
2. Run `knowledge_search` for prior SOWs of the same engagement type and the org's approved SOW template/legal clauses; reuse standard language for assumptions, change-control, and acceptance.
3. Draft the SOW: scope statement, itemized deliverables (each with a definition of done), a milestone schedule mapped to dates/payment triggers, assumptions, explicit exclusions, and per-deliverable acceptance criteria with a review/sign-off window.
4. Hand off the draft marking every unverified field (dates, fees, names) as TBD; state assumptions inline and route to deal owner + legal for sign-off. Do not present as final or send to the client.

# Notes

Wrong if deliverables lack measurable acceptance criteria, if dates/fees are fabricated to fill gaps, or if exclusions are omitted (scope creep risk). Always anchor commercial and legal terms to retrieved precedent, not memory. This is a draft for human legal review — never bind the company by sending or signing. Not for change orders to an existing SOW or for pure pricing quotes.
