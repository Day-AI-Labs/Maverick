# What this skill does

Analyzes a customer contract under the ASC 606 five-step model and recommends how and when revenue should be recognized. Produces a memo that identifies the performance obligations, allocates the transaction price, and states the timing (point-in-time vs. over-time) with cited reasoning.

# Steps

1. Read the contract and any side agreements from the source provided; never assume terms not in the document. Use knowledge_search to pull the governing ASC 606 guidance and the entity's own revenue policy, and cite the specific subtopic/policy clause for each conclusion.
2. Work the five steps in order: (1) identify the contract and confirm criteria are met; (2) identify each distinct performance obligation; (3) determine the transaction price including variable consideration, financing, and constraint; (4) allocate the price to each obligation on a relative standalone-selling-price basis; (5) determine recognition timing per obligation (over time if an ASC 606-25-27 criterion is met, otherwise at the point control transfers).
3. Flag the judgment areas explicitly: variable-consideration estimates and constraint, material rights, principal-vs-agent, contract modifications, and significant financing components. For each, state the position taken and the evidence, and mark anything not supported by the contract or guidance as a judgment requiring review.
4. Report the deliverable: an ASC 606 memo listing the performance obligations, the price allocation, the recognition pattern and timing per obligation, and citations to guidance and contract clauses. State assumptions and hand off for technical-accounting sign-off.

# Notes

The output is wrong if a conclusion is asserted without a citation to the contract clause or ASC 606 paragraph, or if standalone selling prices are fabricated rather than estimated from observable or documented evidence — flag estimated SSPs as unverified. Common traps: treating a non-distinct deliverable as a separate obligation, ignoring the variable-consideration constraint, and missing a material right (e.g. renewal options). This produces a DRAFT recommendation only; revenue accounting policy and journal entries are an irreversible filing matter decided and signed off by a qualified accountant. Do not use for entities reporting under IFRS 15 without confirming the differences.
