# What this skill does

Prepares a deal-specific objection-handling guide: the likely objections (price, timing, incumbent, security, ROI doubt, status-quo bias), each paired with an acknowledge-reframe-prove response and the supporting evidence. The output equips a rep to respond calmly with proof instead of improvising.

# Steps

1. Pull the deal context and known concerns from `knowledge_search` over discovery notes, CRM, and prior emails; pull the team's approved rebuttals, proof assets, and competitive battlecards from the knowledge base. Distinguish objections the prospect actually raised from ones you are anticipating.
2. For each objection, identify the real underlying concern (e.g. "too expensive" often means "I don't see the ROI" or "I lack budget authority"). Do not answer the surface words; answer the root.
3. Draft each response as acknowledge (validate the concern) -> reframe (shift to the value/risk lens) -> prove (a specific metric, customer outcome, or capability), and attach the cited proof. Add a clarifying question to use when the root concern is ambiguous.
4. Assemble the guide grouped by objection type, with a quick-reference table and escalation notes for objections requiring SE/legal/finance. Report it and flag any rebuttal that relies on an unverified claim so it is not used as fact.

# Notes

The guide is wrong if a rebuttal is combative, dismisses the concern, or cites a proof point that cannot be traced to a source — every metric or customer claim must be cited or marked unverified. It is a preparation draft; a human rep decides what to say live and never commits to discounts, contractual terms, or security assurances without the owning team. Do not use it to fabricate competitive claims about rivals; route comparative assertions through approved battlecards only.
