# What this skill does

Assesses whether a ground-up or value-add development project is financially feasible. Produces a feasibility study that compares total development cost against stabilized value and net sale/refi proceeds, returning yield-on-cost, development margin (profit on cost), levered IRR, and a risk view across cost, timing, and exit assumptions.

# Steps

1. Gather the grounded inputs: site/acquisition cost, hard and soft costs, contingency, a development/lease-up timeline, construction-loan terms (LTC, rate, draw schedule), and the stabilized income basis (achievable rents, vacancy, opex). Flag every assumed market input (rents, exit cap, cost PSF, absorption pace) as UNVERIFIED with its source.
2. In the spreadsheet, build total development cost including financing carry over the draw schedule, then derive stabilized NOI and stabilized value (NOI / exit cap). Compute yield-on-cost (stabilized NOI / total cost) and development margin (value less cost, over cost).
3. Model the equity cash-flow timeline — equity draws during construction, lease-up cash flow, and the exit (sale net of costs, or refinance) — and solve levered IRR and equity multiple. Compare yield-on-cost against the market exit cap; the spread is the development premium and the core feasibility test.
4. Run sensitivity over the project killers (hard-cost overrun %, lease-up delay months, exit cap movement) on margin and IRR. State the highest-and-best-use rationale if alternatives were weighed. Report the metrics, the yield-vs-cap spread, and the risk grid; hand off the workbook with all assumptions listed.

# Notes

Output is wrong if construction-loan interest carry is omitted, if contingency is left out of total cost, or if stabilized value uses today's cap rate without testing exit-cap expansion (the dominant risk on a multi-year build). A thin yield-on-cost over the exit cap means the deal does not pencil — say so plainly rather than tuning assumptions until IRR clears a hurdle. This is a go/no-go decision draft: a human sponsor approves committing capital, and the cost basis must be confirmed against a real GC estimate before reliance. Use real-estate-proforma instead for already-stabilized acquisitions with no construction.
