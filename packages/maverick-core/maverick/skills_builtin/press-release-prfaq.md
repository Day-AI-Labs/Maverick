# What this skill does

Frames a proposed product, feature, or initiative by working backwards from the customer: produces a PR-FAQ — a mock launch-day press release plus an anticipated Q&A covering customer, internal, and risk questions. The output forces clarity on who benefits and how before any build commitment.

# Steps

1. Capture the idea from real inputs — the brief, problem statement, target customer, and any market or usage data. Use `knowledge_search` to retrieve supporting evidence (market size, customer pain, comparable launches) and cite it. Name the specific customer and the problem; if either is vague, surface that gap rather than papering over it.
2. Draft the mock press release as if it shipped: headline, subhead, the customer problem, the solution, a leader quote, a customer quote, and how to get started. Keep it concrete and benefit-led; no internal jargon, no metrics you cannot source.
3. Write the FAQ in two sections — external (what customers ask: price, availability, how it works, why it beats alternatives) and internal (why now, what it costs, what could go wrong, dependencies, what we are NOT doing). Answer the hard tradeoff questions honestly.
4. Hand off the PR-FAQ with the open questions and assumptions called out explicitly, and mark any unsupported claim (adoption, savings, market size) as UNVERIFIED so it is validated before resourcing.

# Notes

The PR-FAQ is wrong if the press release reads as aspirational fiction with invented adoption or savings numbers, or if the FAQ dodges the real risks — the document exists to expose weak ideas early, not to sell them. A press release nobody would care to read is the signal to kill or rework the idea. This skill drafts a framing artifact; it does not approve the project, set pricing, or authorize a launch — leadership decides. Do not treat its quotes or metrics as real until sourced.
