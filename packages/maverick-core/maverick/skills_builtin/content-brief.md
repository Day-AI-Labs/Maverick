# What this skill does

Produces a production-ready brief for a single piece of content (blog post, landing page, whitepaper, email, video script): the target audience, the angle/thesis, an SEO/intent frame, a section-by-section outline, and the CTA. Gives a writer enough to draft without re-researching. It briefs; it does not write the final asset or publish it.

# Steps

1. Capture the inputs: topic, format, funnel stage, and primary goal. Use `knowledge_search` to pull the ICP/persona, brand voice/style guide, target keyword or search intent, competing/related existing content, and any product claims that must stay accurate. Note the as-of date and flag missing inputs.
2. Define the audience and ANGLE: who it's for, the pain it addresses, the single thesis, and what makes it differentiated from existing pieces found in step 1. Mark any product or stat claim that needs source verification before publication.
3. Build the outline: working title, hook, H2/H3 section structure with one-line intent per section, required proof points (data, quotes, examples — cited or flagged as needed), and target length. Keep it skimmable and aligned to the search intent.
4. Specify the CTA (offer, placement, link target) and SEO frame (primary keyword, meta description draft). Report the brief, stating assumptions and listing any unverified claims to confirm; hand off to the writer — do not draft or publish the asset.

# Notes

Wrong if it invents stats, customer quotes, or product capabilities — every factual claim is cited or marked "verify before publishing." A brief that ignores the brand style guide or duplicates existing content wastes the draft cycle; always check for overlap. Don't use to write the finished piece (this is the spec, not the draft) or for purely visual/design asks with no copy. Publishing is a human decision.
