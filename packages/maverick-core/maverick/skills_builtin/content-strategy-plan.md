# What this skill does

Builds a documented content strategy for a brand or product: audience, content pillars, formats, channels, and a publishing cadence. Output is a strategy doc that maps each pillar to a target audience and business goal, lists the formats and channels per pillar, and proposes a realistic cadence given stated resourcing. Handles inbound/brand content programs; it is the plan, not the individual pieces.

# Steps

1. Gather the real inputs via knowledge_search: business goals, ICP/audience, existing content inventory, brand voice, and team capacity. If any are missing, list them as open questions rather than assuming values.
2. Derive 3-5 content pillars, each tied to a specific audience segment and a business goal (awareness, activation, retention) grounded in the gathered inputs — not generic themes.
3. For each pillar specify formats (blog, video, newsletter, case study) and the channels they ship to, and map an example topic from the existing inventory or a clear gap in it.
4. Propose a cadence calibrated to stated team capacity (pieces per pillar per month, owners, review gates) and report the strategy with assumptions and open questions flagged for the marketing lead to confirm.

# Notes

The plan is wrong if cadence ignores real capacity (over-committing a one-person team) or if pillars are untethered from a goal/audience. Do not invent audience data or inventory — cite what knowledge_search returns and mark gaps as unverified. This is a recommendation for a human owner to ratify and resource; do not schedule or publish anything. Not for one-off content briefs or single-asset requests — use a briefing skill for those.
