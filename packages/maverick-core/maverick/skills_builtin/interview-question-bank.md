# What this skill does

Builds a behavioral interview question bank for a role: a set of structured (STAR-style) questions, each mapped to a target competency and paired with a scoring rubric and probing follow-ups. Output lets a panel assess consistently against the same criteria rather than ad-hoc.

# Steps

1. Establish the target competencies and level for the role. knowledge_search the role's competency model or JD; if none exists, derive 4-6 competencies to assess from the JD and flag that the model was inferred.
2. For each competency, draft 2-3 behavioral questions that ask for a specific past situation ("Tell me about a time...") rather than hypotheticals or self-assessment, plus 1-2 follow-up probes to push past rehearsed answers toward evidence of action and result.
3. Pair each question with a leveled rubric: what a weak, on-target, and strong answer looks like for the role's level, expressed as observable signals (decisions made, scope owned, outcome) — not vague traits.
4. Hand off the bank as a draft, organized by competency with a coverage matrix (every target competency has ≥1 question), and note assumptions: inferred competencies, questions needing legal/HR review, and that question selection per interviewer is a human decision.

# Notes

The bank is wrong if questions are leading or hypothetical (no behavioral evidence), if rubrics describe personality rather than observable signals, or if a competency is left uncovered. Screen out questions touching protected characteristics (age, family, health, origin) — flag any borderline item for HR review rather than including it. Cite the competency model/JD used; mark inferred competencies. Do not present this as a vetted/legally-cleared instrument — that review is a human step. Not for synthesizing results after interviews (use the evaluation skill).
