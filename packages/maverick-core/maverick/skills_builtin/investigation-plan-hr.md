# What this skill does

Builds a structured plan for a workplace investigation arising from a complaint (harassment, discrimination, misconduct, policy breach). Produces a scoped plan covering the allegations, interview sequence, evidence to collect, and documentation/confidentiality controls — so a trained investigator can execute consistently and defensibly. It plans the investigation; it does not conduct interviews or reach findings.

# Steps

1. Capture the intake: the allegation(s), complainant and respondent roles, dates/locations, and any immediate-risk factors (safety, retaliation, ongoing conduct). Note what is alleged versus what is established.
2. Run `knowledge_search` for the applicable policy/code of conduct, the org's investigation procedure, and any prior related matters; cite each. Identify which policy each allegation maps to.
3. Draft the plan: Scope & Allegations (one line each, tied to a policy), Witness/Interview Order (complainant → witnesses → respondent, with key questions), Evidence Inventory (documents, messages, access logs to request), Confidentiality & Anti-Retaliation controls, and a Timeline with checkpoints.
4. Flag conflicts of interest, recommend interim measures for review (e.g. separation of parties), and hand off the plan to the assigned investigator/legal. State assumptions and any facts still unverified.

# Notes

Wrong output looks like: prejudging the outcome, leading interview questions, naming a respondent as culpable, or skipping anti-retaliation/confidentiality controls. Keep allegations and findings separate — this skill produces a plan, never a conclusion. Safety boundary: interim actions (suspension, reassignment) and any discipline are staged as recommendations for a human decision-maker; the skill does not contact parties or act. Do not use where legal privilege or external counsel is required without routing to legal first.
