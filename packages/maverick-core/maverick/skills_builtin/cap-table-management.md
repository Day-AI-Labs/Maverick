# What this skill does

Maintains and analyzes a capitalization table: fully-diluted ownership, option pool, and the dilution impact of a proposed round or grant.

# Steps

1. Rebuild the fully-diluted base from `spreadsheet`: founders, common, each preferred series, the option pool (granted vs available), warrants, and SAFEs/notes on an as-converted basis.
2. For a proposed round, model pre/post-money, new shares, price per share, and the resulting fully-diluted percentages, including any pool top-up the term sheet requires.
3. Show the waterfall: who owns what before and after, and the dilution each existing holder takes. Flag SAFE/note conversions and any anti-dilution adjustments.
4. Reconcile to the share count of record and surface discrepancies. State assumptions (conversion terms, pool target) and hand off — legal issuance is not this skill's call.

# Notes

Cap tables go wrong on un-converted SAFEs, an option pool measured granted-only, or anti-dilution missed. Always reconcile to the source of record. Actual share issuance and 409A decisions belong to counsel and the board.
