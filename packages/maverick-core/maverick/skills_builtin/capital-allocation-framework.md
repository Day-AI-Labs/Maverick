# What this skill does

Frames a capital-allocation decision by structuring competing investment options against a consistent set of financial and strategic criteria, so a constrained capital budget can be deployed on a defensible basis. Produces a ranked option set with NPV/IRR/payback, risk and strategic-fit scoring, and a recommended allocation under the stated constraint. It frames and recommends the decision; the capital committee approves it.

# Steps

1. Capture each option in the spreadsheet from the inputs provided: required capital, projected cash flows by year, useful life, and the strategic rationale. Record the discount rate (hurdle), the total capital constraint, and any mandatory/non-discretionary items separately; if cash flows or the hurdle rate are assumed rather than given, label them.
2. Compute the financial metrics per option: NPV at the hurdle rate, IRR, discounted payback, and capital efficiency (NPV per dollar invested). Keep formulas transparent so a reviewer can trace each figure to its inputs.
3. Score non-financial dimensions on a stated rubric (strategic fit, execution risk, regulatory/mandatory status, interdependencies) and combine with the financial view — do not let a single high-NPV option mask high execution risk or crowd out a mandatory project.
4. Rank options and propose an allocation that fits the capital constraint (greedy by capital efficiency, then adjusted for mandatory items and dependencies), reporting the ranked table, the funded vs. deferred split, total NPV captured, and the binding constraint — stating every assumption and handing approval to the capital committee.

# Notes

Results are driven by the cash-flow projections and hurdle rate: garbage inputs produce a confidently wrong ranking, so projections must be sourced or explicitly marked as assumptions, and the discount rate must be the agreed hurdle, not invented. NPV-per-dollar ranking can violate constraints or ignore project dependencies — reconcile both before recommending. This is a framing and recommendation tool; capital commitment is irreversible and belongs to the committee, so stage the recommendation, never authorize spend. Do not use for routine sustaining/below-threshold capex or where options are non-comparable in horizon without normalizing.
