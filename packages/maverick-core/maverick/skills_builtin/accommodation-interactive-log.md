# What this skill does

This skill structures the good-faith interactive-process record that the ADA and the Pregnant Workers Fairness Act (PWFA) require when an employee requests a reasonable accommodation: it captures the request, each step of the back-and-forth dialogue, the options considered, the essential-function analysis, the undue-hardship assessment, and the outcome, with deadline tracking at each stage. It builds a defensible contemporaneous log — the record that demonstrates the employer engaged in good faith — while keeping medical information sealed and out of the general personnel file. The output is a structured process log and next-step checklist for the assigned HR/leave owner; it does not grant, deny, or communicate any decision.

# Steps

1. Use knowledge_search to pull the org's accommodation policy, the relevant essential-function/job description, the ADA/PWFA interactive-process checklist, and applicable deadlines, so the log fields match the legal framework (request -> dialogue -> options -> essential-function analysis -> undue-hardship -> outcome).
2. Record the request: date received, the limitation/need at a functional level (NOT the underlying diagnosis), and what accommodation was requested. Place any supporting medical documentation in a separate, access-restricted medical record — never in the body of this log or the personnel file.
3. Log each interactive-process step with date, participants, options discussed, and the functional/essential-function and undue-hardship reasoning for each option, so the dialogue trail is contemporaneous and complete. Track the response-due date at every stage and flag any approaching or missed deadline.
4. Assemble the structured log (timeline, options matrix, deadlines, open items) plus a next-step checklist for the HR/leave owner, and stage it. Mark that the grant/deny decision and any communication to the employee are reserved to the human owner — this skill documents the process, it does not decide or notify.

# Notes

Medical data is sealed: capture functional limitations and accommodation needs, never the diagnosis, and route any medical documents to a confidential file segregated from personnel records — mishandling this is itself a violation. Good faith is shown by the contemporaneous trail, so log dates and who-said-what at each step, not just the conclusion. Note the PWFA difference (pregnancy-related limitations need not rise to an ADA disability) and the predictable-assessment categories — don't apply a pure-ADA frame to a PWFA request. This skill never grants, denies, or communicates an accommodation; it stages the record and flags deadlines for the human owner. An undue-hardship conclusion is the employer's legal call, not the agent's — surface the cost/operational facts, don't declare hardship.
