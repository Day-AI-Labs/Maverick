# What this skill does

Qualifies research spend for the federal R&D tax credit (IRC Section 41) by applying the four-part test to each project and assembling qualified research expenses (QREs). Produces a qualification memo with a per-project four-part-test conclusion and a QRE schedule (wages, supplies, contract research) ready for credit computation.

# Steps

1. Inventory candidate projects and pull cost data via `spreadsheet`: employee time by project, supply/materials consumed, and contractor invoices. Gather contemporaneous evidence (tickets, design docs, test logs) that shows experimentation, not routine work.
2. Apply the four-part test per project via `knowledge_search` for the controlling rules: (a) permitted purpose — new/improved business component function, performance, reliability, or quality; (b) technological in nature — relies on hard sciences; (c) elimination of uncertainty — capability, method, or design was uncertain; (d) process of experimentation — evaluation of alternatives. Record the evidence supporting each prong and exclude funded research, post-production work, and pure aesthetics.
3. Compute QREs for qualifying projects: qualified W-2 wages (direct + supervision + support, with the substantially-all 80% rule for partly-qualified staff), supplies consumed in research, and contract research at the 65% statutory limitation. Exclude capital items, overhead, and travel.
4. Assemble the memo: per-project four-part conclusion with cited evidence, the QRE schedule by category, and the total qualified base. Note whether the regular or ASC method and any Section 174 capitalization interaction applies. Report the qualified amount and flag weakly-documented projects; stage the credit claim for a tax professional and do not file.

# Notes

Output is wrong if routine engineering, configuration, or market research is claimed, if the 65% contract-research and 80% substantially-all limits are skipped, or if QREs lack contemporaneous documentation (the IRS disallows undocumented credits and the claim now requires Section 41 detail on amended returns). Funded research (where the customer bears the cost or keeps rights) is excluded — verify contract terms. This is a qualification draft, not a filed credit: the amount, base-period mechanics, and any payroll-tax offset election are irreversible filing positions for a qualified preparer. Do not use for state credits without confirming the state's own rules, or where projects are pure software UI/cosmetic work failing the technological prong.
