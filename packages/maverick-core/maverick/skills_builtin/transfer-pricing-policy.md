# What this skill does

Documents a defensible transfer-pricing (TP) policy for a specified intercompany transaction (tangible goods, services, IP/royalty, or intra-group financing). Produces a TP policy memo that names the controlled transaction, selects and justifies a pricing method, and lays out the supporting economic analysis at an arm's-length range. Output is a draft for tax/legal review, not a filed position.

# Steps

1. Pull the transaction facts from the engagement record: the legal entities involved, their jurisdictions, the function/asset/risk (FAR) split, the transaction type, and volumes/amounts. Do not invent counterparties or amounts — flag any missing fact rather than assuming it.
2. Use knowledge_search against the applicable guidance (OECD TP Guidelines, local-country TP rules, any prior TP study or APA on file) to identify the candidate methods (CUP, RPM, cost-plus, TNMM/CPM, profit split) and which fits the tested party's FAR profile. Cite each source by name and section.
3. Select the method, name the tested party and profit-level indicator, and state the arm's-length range. If comparables data is referenced, mark whether it is verified from a benchmarking set or is an unverified placeholder pending a comparables search.
4. Draft the TP policy memo (transaction description, method selection rationale, FAR analysis, economic analysis/range, intercompany agreement references) and hand off to the tax lead, stating every assumption and every gap that needs documentation support.

# Notes

The memo is wrong if it states a method without tying it to the tested party's FAR profile, or quotes an arm's-length range with no comparable basis — always mark unbenchmarked ranges as unverified. Cite the specific guidance section; never paraphrase OECD/local rules from memory. This is a draft recommendation: method selection and any filed TP position are decided by qualified tax counsel. Do not use for purely domestic transactions with no cross-border or intercompany element, or where no controlled relationship exists.
