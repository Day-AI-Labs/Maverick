# What this skill does

Builds a SWOT (strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, threats) and a Porter's Five Forces analysis where every cell is backed by a dated source, producing a strategic snapshot that is evidence-based rather than vibes-based. The goal class is "assess competitive/strategic position rigorously": no unsourced assertions, internal factors (S/W) kept distinct from external (O/T), and each of the five forces rated with reasoning.

# Steps

1. Define the unit of analysis (company, product, business line) and gather evidence with knowledge_search for internal facts and web_search for market/competitor facts, recording sources and dates.
2. Populate SWOT, keeping strengths/weaknesses to internal, controllable factors and opportunities/threats to external, environmental factors. Cite each cell; drop any item you cannot source.
3. Work the Five Forces (rivalry, new entrants, substitutes, buyer power, supplier power), rating each high/medium/low with the specific evidence that drives the rating.
4. Synthesize: identify the two or three forces or factors that most constrain or enable the strategy, and note where the evidence is thin so the reader knows the soft spots.

# Notes

The common error is putting an external market trend in "strengths" or an internal capability in "opportunities" — keep the internal/external axis clean or the framework adds no insight. Unsourced cells turn the analysis into opinion; if you cannot cite it, do not assert it. A force rated without reasoning is just a label; show the evidence. Market data ages fast, so date every external source. This skill produces an analytical artifact for review; it does not set strategy or commit the organization to any move.
