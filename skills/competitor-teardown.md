# What this skill does

Runs a repeatable, sourced, dated teardown of a competitor or rival product covering positioning, pricing, feature gaps, and public claims, so competitive intelligence is consistent and verifiable rather than anecdotal. The goal class is "profile a competitor defensibly": same structure every time, every fact dated and sourced, claims separated from verified facts.

# Steps

1. Fix the target and the dimensions: positioning/ICP, pricing/packaging, feature set vs ours, go-to-market, and notable public claims. Gather evidence with web_search (public site, pricing pages, docs, reviews) and knowledge_search (internal intel), recording source and date for each.
2. Map positioning and pricing from primary sources (their own pages) first; mark anything from a secondary source (analyst, review, rumor) as such and lower its weight.
3. Build a feature-gap table comparing their capabilities to ours, distinguishing confirmed features from marketing claims you could not verify. Flag claims that look like puffery.
4. Synthesize the two or three places they are genuinely strong and the two or three real gaps, dating the whole snapshot so a reader knows when it was true.

# Notes

Competitor pages are marketing, not ground truth — separate a verified feature from a claimed one, and never treat a competitor's own benchmark as neutral. Competitive intel rots fast; an undated teardown will be quoted as current long after it is stale, so date everything. Cherry-picking only their weaknesses produces a flattering but useless analysis; report their genuine strengths too. This skill produces an internal intelligence artifact for review; it does not make public claims, contact the competitor, or set pricing.
